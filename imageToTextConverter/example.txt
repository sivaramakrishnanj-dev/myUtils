2.  **Logic:**
* It uses `pytesseract.image_to_data` instead of just `image_to_string`. This gives us the pixel height of every word.
* It calculates the **Median Height** of all words to find the "body text" size.
* It groups words into lines.
* If a line's text is significantly larger (e.g., 1.3x or 1.8x) than the median, it prepends `#` or `##` to make it a Markdown header.
